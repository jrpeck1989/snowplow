General configuration and parametersâ€‹

Initializationâ€‹

Assuming you have completed theÂ Lua Tracker SetupÂ for your Lua project, you are now ready to initialize the Lua Tracker.

Required headersâ€‹

Make sure you have the following includes:

#include <Ethernet.h>

Initialize SnowplowTrackerâ€‹

After your includes but before yourÂ setup()Â function, initialize yourÂ SnowplowTrackerÂ something like this:

Setting the endpointâ€‹

Endpoint refers to the location of your collector: you need to point your Arduino tracker to your collector endpoint, to ensure that data generated by your Arduino is logged by the collector.

You can set the collector endpoint for the Cloudfront collector by adding to yourÂ setup()Â function:

If you are running a different collector (not the Cloudfront collector) then add to yourÂ setup()Â function:

Tracking custom structured eventsâ€‹

Custom structured events are the only form of tracking currently supported by the Snowplow Arduino tracker. Whenever you want to record an event or sensor reading from your IP-connected Arduino, useÂ trackStructEventÂ to send this data to Snowplow.

Some examples of tracking custom structured events from your Arduino board(s) might include:

  • Monitoring the environment (temperature, humidity, light levels etc) in your warehouse/factory/workplace/shop/museum
  • Tracking the movement of products around your shop/warehouse/factory using Arduino,Â RFID readersÂ and Snowplow
  • Sending vehicle fleet information (locations, speeds, fuel levels etc) back to Snowplow using Arduino'sÂ 3G and GPSÂ shields

Setup debuggingâ€‹

By default, debug logging to your Arduino Serial Monitor console is switchedÂ onÂ for the Snowplow Arduino Tracker, which should help you to identify any problems debugging your Snowplow event tracking.

To switch off this logging when you are finished testing, edit this line found near the top of your copy ofÂ SnowplowTracker.cpp:

#define LOG_LEVEL   0x03 // Change to 0x00 when you've finished testing

As the comment says, change "0x03" to "0x00" to switch off all logging to your Arduino Serial Monitor console.

The full set of logging levels are as follows:
